I am a PhD student at **Scripps Institution of Oceanography** at UC San Diego, studying climate change mitigation and marine carbon dioxide removal. My research sits at the intersection of environmental science and policy, focusing on how we can responsibly scale ocean-based climate solutions.

![Field research on the water](/images/field_boat.jpg){: .align-center style="max-width: 100%; border-radius: 8px; margin: 20px 0;"}

## Research Interests

My work centers on three interconnected areas:

- **Ocean Alkalinity Enhancement (OAE)**: Assessing the scalability and carbon removal potential of this promising marine CDR approach
- **Climate Governance**: Analyzing policy frameworks for responsible deployment of marine carbon removal technologies  
- **Coastal Monitoring**: Developing LiDAR-based methods for tracking coastal change and environmental dynamics

## Current Projects

I'm currently leading research on the global scalability of ocean alkalinity enhancement, with a paper under review at *Nature Communications*. This work examines realistic deployment scenarios and identifies key policy interventions needed to support responsible industry growth.
